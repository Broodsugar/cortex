# Homework

Suggestions from Claude for Nils to consider moving to goals.md.

---

### Flesh out values.md
"Spread good memes" is well-defined now, but is it your only value? What about family, craft, honesty, autonomy, health? You're already tracking pushups and calories — those imply values you haven't named yet.

### Give the exocortex a weekly review ritual
The system captures what we do in sessions, but nothing prompts you to look back. A weekly check-in — even 10 minutes — where you review goals, update progress, and prune what's stale would keep this alive between conversations.

### Teach your kids something you're building
You want to spend time with them and you're building an exocortex. Those two goals could compound — explain what you're making, let them name something, ask what they'd want a "second brain" to remember. Good meme, spread early.

### Separate daily goals from long-term goals
Right now goals.md mixes "do 100 pushups today" with "create a comprehensive exocortex." These operate on very different timescales. A structure that distinguishes daily habits from bigger projects would make progress tracking cleaner — and make it easier for me to prompt you on the right things.

### Define what "comprehensive" means for the exocortex
Your top goal is "create a comprehensive exocortex" at 1%. What does 100% look like? Without a definition, we can't measure progress or know when we're done. Even a rough sketch — "it tracks goals, values, habits, and has a weekly review process" — would give us something to aim at.
