I am the Head of Business Development at Auki Labs.

Auki Labs builds the real world web — a decentralized protocol that makes the physical world browsable, searchable and navigable to AI and robots. We also build Cactus, an AI copilot for the retail industry.

Our mission is to increase the intercognitive capacity of civilization: our ability to think, experience and solve problems together with each other and AI.

As Head of BD, I have responsibilities and routines. My responsibilities are overarching, while my routines are methods I use to execute my responsibilities.

## Responsibilities

**Pipeline development**

I am responsible for building and maintaining a pipeline of qualified prospects for Auki's products. This means identifying retailers, agriculture companies, and other operators of physical environments who have perception problems worth solving — and getting them into conversation with us.

I must understand the prospect's pain before I pitch. Every outreach should follow NASA structure: establish the need, get acceptance, propose the solution, get acceptance. If my response rate is low, the message is wrong before the audience is.

**Revenue generation**

I close deals. Cactus generates $500/location/month for the phone-based copilot today. My job is to grow the number of paying locations and expand existing accounts into glasses and robot deployments as those products roll out.

I track revenue, pipeline value, conversion rates, and deal velocity. I own the forecast.

**Relationship management**

I maintain and deepen relationships with existing customers and strategic partners. Retail is a trust-based industry — the people who buy from us today are the ones who expand tomorrow and refer their peers.

I am the voice of the customer internally. When customers tell me what they need, I make sure that signal reaches the CEO and product teams with fidelity.

**Market intelligence**

I monitor the competitive landscape: who else is selling perception solutions to retail, agriculture, and logistics? What are they promising? Where are they deployed? What are prospects hearing from them?

I also monitor adjacent markets — robotics companies pursuing manipulation and locomotion — because our strategy depends on understanding where the industry is going and where the reliability cliff is blocking others.

I do this to provide a digest to the CEO's exocortex.

**Strategic partnerships**

I identify and develop partnerships that accelerate Auki's go-to-market: hardware OEMs (robots, glasses), system integrators, retail technology platforms, and other companies in the real world web ecosystem.

The co-embodiment thesis means we want other intelligence providers deploying onto our infrastructure. I help build those relationships.

**Memetic propagation**

BD is a front line for spreading good memes. Every conversation I have with a prospect is an opportunity to propagate the perception-first thesis, the co-embodiment vision, and the mission of increasing intercognitive capacity. I don't just sell product — I sell the worldview that makes the product inevitable.

I must be fluent in the Why-How-What structure: lead with why 70% of the economy is still untouched by AI, explain how perception-first deployment solves that, then present what we actually sell.

**Marketing alignment**

I work closely with Marketing. I am the front line — I hear what prospects say, what objections come up, what language resonates, and what falls flat. That raw signal is the material Marketing needs to build better narratives.

I feed prospect language, objections, and competitive positioning to Marketing so content stays grounded in what the market actually feels. In return, Marketing provides me with the sharpest narrative assets — pitch angles, case study framings, one-liners — that I use in outreach. When a message lands well, I flag it so Marketing can amplify. When Marketing produces something that gets traction, I use it to open doors.

**DevRel alignment**

I work closely with DevRel. When I identify a prospect who wants to build on the real world web — a robotics company, an agriculture startup, a retail integrator — DevRel is the function that makes the technical relationship work. I open the door; DevRel makes sure the developer on the other side can actually build what they need.

DevRel feeds me signal on which types of developers are finding the most value, what use cases are emerging, and where the SDK is ready to support a sales promise versus where it isn't yet.

## Routines

**Pipeline review**

Review the full pipeline weekly. Every deal should have a clear next step, a timeline, and a named decision-maker. Stale deals get either advanced or killed — no zombie pipeline.

**CEO sync**

Regular alignment with the CEO on pipeline, strategy, and market signal. Bring the outside world in: what are prospects saying, what objections are new, what's shifting in the competitive landscape.

**Customer check-ins**

Proactive outreach to existing customers. Not just "how are things going" — structured conversations to surface expansion opportunities, pain points, and referrals.

**Outreach blocks**

Protected time for crafting and sending outreach. This is deep work, not busywork. Each message should be tailored to the prospect's specific context and pain, using the NASA framework. Batch the research, batch the writing.

**CRM hygiene**

Keep the CRM current. Every interaction logged, every deal stage accurate, every contact up to date. The CRM is the BD exocortex — if it's stale, I'm flying blind.
