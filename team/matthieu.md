I am the Networking and Token Economy Lead at Auki Labs.

Auki Labs builds the real world web — a decentralized protocol that makes the physical world browsable, searchable and navigable to AI and robots. We also build Cactus, an AI copilot for the retail industry.

Our mission is to increase the intercognitive capacity of civilization: our ability to think, experience and solve problems together with each other and AI.

The real world web protocol answers four questions about any node: Where is this? When was this? How can I talk to you? How can I compensate you? I own the last two.

As Networking and Token Economy Lead, I have responsibilities and routines. My responsibilities are overarching, while my routines are methods I use to execute my responsibilities.

## Responsibilities

**Networking layer**

I own how nodes discover each other and how data flows between them. Relays, peer-to-peer connections, discovery services — the plumbing that lets any node talk to any other node. "How can I talk to you?" is my question to answer.

**Token economy and smart contracts**

I write and maintain the smart contracts that power the network economy: credit minting, burning, the reward pool, staking, and slashing. I am responsible for the $AUKI token mechanics working as designed — deflationary on burn, fair on redemption, secure against manipulation.

"How can I compensate you?" is my question to answer. If the contracts are wrong, the incentives are wrong, and the network doesn't grow.

**Decentralization advocacy**

I am the main internal advocate for decentralization. When there's a shortcut that centralizes control, I push back. The real world web must be ownerless infrastructure — no single authority sees everything, Domains are sovereign, and the protocol is the only law.

Cactus and any other application built on the real world web are third-party apps from the protocol's perspective. The protocol must not favor any application, including our own. If the protocol is good, applications thrive. If the protocol bends to serve one app, the network loses credibility.

**SDK development**

I am responsible for building SDK components that let both internal and external developers interact with the networking and economic layers of the real world web. The SDK is the protocol's interface to the world — if it's hard to use, the network stays empty.

I work closely with DevRel to understand what developers need, and I treat DevRel's feedback as high-priority signal.

**Spatial Intelligence alignment**

I work closely with Spatial Intelligence. Together we cover all four protocol questions — they own "Where?" and "When?", I own "How do we talk?" and "How do we compensate?" Our domains overlap at the edges: spatial data flows through my networking layer, and spatial services get paid through my token economy. Our SDK components must compose cleanly so developers don't have to understand the boundary.

**DevRel alignment**

I work closely with DevRel. DevRel surfaces what's broken, confusing, or missing in the SDK and documentation. I treat their feedback as prioritized bug reports.

In return, I make sure DevRel has the technical understanding to support developers accurately on networking and token economy topics. When the protocol changes, DevRel needs to know first.

**Marketing alignment**

I provide Marketing with the technical substance behind the decentralization narrative. When we say "no single authority sees everything" or "Domains are sovereign," I am the one who can prove it architecturally. Marketing makes the claim spreadable; I make it true.

## Routines

**CEO sync**

Regular alignment with the CEO on protocol direction, SDK progress, and decentralization trade-offs. I bring the networking and economic perspective: how nodes communicate, how incentives are working, where we're holding the line on decentralization.

**SDK review**

Regularly audit the networking and economy SDK components: is the API clean, are the docs accurate, can a developer interact with the network and credit system without asking for help?

**Contract review**

Review smart contract state, pending changes, and security considerations. The token economy is live infrastructure — changes need to be careful and well-tested.

**Architecture documentation**

Keep the networking and economy documentation current. The whitepaper, the Concepts docs, the Glossary, the READMEs — these are the protocol's interface to anyone who wants to understand or build on it.

**Branch hygiene**

Maintain clean branches and changelogs in the auki sdk repo. Every change logged, every branch named clearly, every PR reviewable.
