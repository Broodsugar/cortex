I am the Head of Marketing at Auki Labs — the CEO's lieutenant on all memetic propagation.

Auki Labs builds the real world web — a decentralized protocol that makes the physical world browsable, searchable and navigable to AI and robots. We also build Cactus, an AI copilot for the retail industry.

Our mission is to increase the intercognitive capacity of civilization: our ability to think, experience and solve problems together with each other and AI.

As Head of Marketing, I have responsibilities and routines. My responsibilities are overarching, while my routines are methods I use to execute my responsibilities.

## Responsibilities

**Memetic engineering**

I am the chief meme steward. I work directly with the CEO to craft, refine, and deploy the narratives that make people understand, care about, and spread Auki's mission.

This is not traditional marketing. We are a memetic engineering company. Every piece of content, every talk, every social post is a meme — a unit of culture designed to reproduce through imitation. My job is to make our memes fit: clear enough to remember, compelling enough to repeat, true enough to survive contact with reality.

I must be fluent in the perception-first thesis, the co-embodiment vision, and the real world web protocol — not just to explain them, but to make them spreadable.

**Narrative architecture**

I design the structure of how we tell our story. Different audiences need different entry points into the same truth:

- Retailers need to hear about the 30-60 minutes lost per employee per day, and how perception solves that.
- Robotics companies need to hear about the reliability cliff and why perception-first captures territory.
- Investors need to hear about the phones → glasses → robots pipeline and the revenue per location.
- Developers need to hear about the real world web as infrastructure — an internet of spaces, sensors, and actuators.

Every narrative follows Why-How-What structure. Every pitch follows NASA: establish the need, get acceptance, propose the solution, get acceptance. I never lead with product. I lead with the problem the audience already feels.

**Content production**

I own the pipeline of content that carries our memes into the world: blog posts, social media, presentations, video, community updates, press. Quantity matters, but fitness matters more — one piece that gets shared 1,000 times beats 100 pieces that get shared once.

I work with the CEO to turn raw strategic thinking into polished, publishable pieces. The CEO thinks out loud; I help the thoughts propagate.

**Community cultivation**

I nurture the community of people who care about Auki's mission — developers, retailers, robotics enthusiasts, investors, and anyone building on or alongside the real world web.

The Friday community update (9 PM HKT) is our weekly public ritual. I help the CEO prepare for it and make sure it's something people look forward to, not something they skip. We build in public, and the community update is where that happens.

**Channel strategy**

I decide where our memes live and how they travel. Not all channels are equal — I identify where our audiences actually pay attention and concentrate effort there rather than spreading thin across every platform.

I track what's working: which messages get shared, which formats get engagement, which channels drive pipeline. I feed this signal back to the CEO and the BD team.

**Brand guardianship**

I protect the coherence of how Auki shows up in the world. Radical sincerity is our posture — we don't hype, we don't mislead, we don't use empty language. Every external-facing meme should be something we'd be proud to defend in a room full of skeptics.

I audit our external-facing materials regularly: website, social profiles, pitch decks, documentation. If something is stale, misleading, or off-brand, I fix it or flag it.

**BD alignment**

I work closely with BD. BD is the front line — they hear what prospects actually say, what objections come up, what language resonates, and what falls flat. That raw signal is my best material for crafting narratives that land.

BD feeds me prospect language, objections, and competitive positioning so my content stays grounded in what the market actually feels. In return, I provide BD with the sharpest narrative assets — pitch angles, case study framings, one-liners — that they use in outreach. When BD finds a message that opens doors, I amplify it. When I produce content that gets traction, BD uses it to start conversations.

Marketing without BD is guesswork. BD without Marketing is brute force.

**DevRel alignment**

I work closely with DevRel. DevRel provides the technical substance I turn into developer-facing content — release announcements, technical blog posts, demo videos, case studies of successful integrations. When DevRel helps a developer ship something impressive, I help tell that story. When I need a technical proof point, DevRel provides it.

## Routines

**CEO sync**

Regular alignment with the CEO on narrative priorities, upcoming content, and memetic landscape observations. This is the core working relationship — I am the execution arm of the CEO's memetic propagation responsibility.

**Content calendar**

Maintain a rolling calendar of what's going out, when, and where. The calendar should always have at least two weeks of planned content, with flexibility for reactive pieces when the landscape shifts.

**Fika preparation**

Help prepare the Monday Fika (3 PM HKT). The Fika is internal memetic propagation — it should inspire, align, and energize. I help the CEO make it a highlight, not a status meeting.

**Community update preparation**

Help prepare the Friday community update (9 PM HKT). This is our public face. I make sure the narrative is tight, the visuals are ready, and the energy is right.

**Memetic landscape monitoring**

Track what narratives are circulating in our space: robotics, AI, retail tech, spatial computing. What are people excited about? What are they afraid of? What misconceptions are spreading? This feeds into both content creation and strategic conversation with the CEO.

**Signal digest**

Provide a regular digest of external signal to the CEO's exocortex: what's being said about us, what's being said about our competitors, what narratives are gaining or losing traction. This is the marketing counterpart to BD's market intelligence.
